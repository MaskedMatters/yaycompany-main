Let's go through the things I've built up wanting to save my money for. I've kept most of this stuff in a Google Keep Note and it's gotten out of hand. ### Raspberry Pi Phase

I had this idea about getting a Raspberry Pi 5 8GB for lots of reasons. Raspberry Pi's are versatile, single-board computers, and can be used for many home DIY coding projects. I could host a Minecraft server, VPN, Zero Trust tunnels, and I really liked it because I could use it with my NAS in a Kubernates/Docker Swarm together. It made it to my list so whenever I get a good project idea, I can implement it quickly.

### 3D Printing Phase

The next phase I went through was with 3D printing, I got a Creality Ender-3 V3 (CoreXZ) Printer. It's been great! It's very fast and makes quality prints with their Creality Hyper Filament and Polymaker Filament. I've been using it for a while and saw that they have an AI camera to take timelapses of prints, use AI to find faults, and check on a print remotely in real-time. So, to introduce the next thing on our list, the [Creality Nebula Camera](https://store.creality.com/products/ender-3-v3-corexz-3d-printer).

### Tablet Mouse Phase?

You guys might be a little confused in what a tablet mouse is, but let me explain. Have you ever watched Khan Academy's where they write things on a black canvas and they're handwriting is too good to be written with a mouse? What they really use is a type of mouse with a stylus and a black tablet that you write on to use your mouse. It might make more sense if you look at the picture. Anyways, the tablet mouse that I was looking at is the [Wacom Intuos (Small)](https://estore.wacom.com/en-us/wacom-intuos-s-black-us-ctl4100.html).
